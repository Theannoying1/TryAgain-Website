The kid and the camera

By Jovit Gabriel N. Estrella

Once Upon a star there lived a boy named Caleb, he was a happy little tot of 6 years old and on his birthday he recived a very special gift.

His very own photo camera! Caleb loved the camera, so much that he took it out everyday snapping photos of anything he could. A picture here, a picture there. Until one day a terrible accident happened.

Caleb didnt sleep a wink, haunted by thoughts of his once perfect camera late into the night. That was when a sudden sound caugh his attention.

And there stood on the other side of Caleb's bedroom window. A large winged creature!

"Hello there young lad!" greeted the creature, "Im the Cipsneed! Your sleep fairy" "Sleep fairy?" said Caleb. "That is correct!" Replied the Cipsneed "My job is to make sure you get a goodnights sleep, but its come to my attention that you havent been resting at all lately. I'd like to know the reason why?" Caleb has never heard of a sleep fairy. "My parents told me not to talk to strangers..." "Strangers? Im no stranger! I know ALL the girls and boys around the globe, I know their parents, their homes, their names. Why, i even know your name! Caleb. Now, would a stranger know your name?" Caleb thought on it for a moment. "No i guess not. If you must know why im awake, its my camera... Its broken." And just like that, tears weld up in Calebs eyes. "Let me see"

Then the Cipsneed had an idea. "Why dont i have this camera fixed for you? Perhaps that would help you sleep easier." "You could do that?" "Of course and i know just the place. A factory in my home, Ill lead you to the portal just follow my voice." And the Cipsneed disappeared into the night... "This way..." Caleb followed close behind the Cipsneed, snapping photos to mark the way incase of getting lost.

"Over here..." *Caleb walks inside the forest...* Atlast Caleb reached a house.

"Seems quite abandoned." poor boy... "And dark..." But there was the Cipsneed voice again beckoning. "The portal to my home world is inside come along..." "I must have my camera fixed." So, inspite of his obvious fright. Caleb marched into the home.

Some weeks later, authorities discovered the basement.

The Rest of the boy was never found.
